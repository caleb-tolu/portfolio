1.1 Design Review

Overview

Sign up flows are one of the first encounter your potential customers have with your product. Aside the fact that first impressions are crucial, it makes a lot of sense to make sign up flows as frictionless as possible, for a better user experience.

A good sign up flow aims to collect data from users in a way that feels natural and gives them a feeling of getting something in return.

Solution

1. Redesigned the homepage to add SIGN UP button instead of PLAY. To add to this, I redesigned the homepage because of a ‚Äúbounce‚Äù report I got from a user. She said ‚Äúwhen I entered the website I just got out‚Äù, insinuating that the homepage was clumsy.

2. Noticed there are 3-step processes to sign up. Some users wouldn‚Äôt be that patient to carry out all steps, especially when the first sign up page talks more about bonuses and don‚Äôt get you to the main reason why you choose to sign up.

So, had to reduce the waiting time for the sign up process by fusing the processes to just 2 screens. The bonus page is not lost, I creatively added it to the 2 page sign up process. The idea is to increase speed in sign up process or, create an illusion of speed. Also, there are too many words to read, almost across all screens. I had suggest there should be more shared understanding and less documentation.

3. The CONTACT US button is not necessary, why because there isn‚Äôt any need for users to contact the company yet because the sign up process shouldn‚Äôt be tedious. CONTACT US button creates panic and gives the impression that something is going wrong already from signing up which is supposed to be a simple process. This button can be on a different page.

4. Noticed I couldn‚Äôt sign up on leovegas.com until I used a vpn. The sign up page didn‚Äôt give a response to that, it just keeps glitching. 5. LeoVegas is data driven, there should be concious call-to-action to collect users as subscribers, and then regular customers.
